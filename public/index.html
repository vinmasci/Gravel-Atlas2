About GravelAtlas

Gravel Atlas is designed to be a tool that helps you navigate to the gravel roads of the world via bike.

We want to revel with you in the wild and wonderful, the choose your own adventure, the hike-a-bike, the type 2 fun and anything else you can dream of.

So go forth and explore and share with us what you find. (Also bugs, share site bugs please).

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I add a route?

• Click the Contribute button at the bottom of the map and select "Draw Route".
• Choose your gravel type and start drawing your route on the map.
• You can change the difficulty of the segment as you draw, just select another colour and continue to draw.
• Elevation data will update as you draw to help you grade your route.

*Please only draw one road / path or lane per save.

What do the different colors mean?

The colours represent different gravel or trail technical difficulty:
Green - Easiest (Smooth hardpack gravel, easy riding).
Yellow - Intermediate (Rougher, slippery, or slightly rocky gravel and/or a moderately steep incline).
Red - Difficult (Technical gravel with slippery sections, washouts, tyre ruts, or large rocks to navigate and/or a very steep incline).
Dark Red - Extreme (Hike-a-bike or MTB territory).
Blue - Rail Trail or Bike Path (Rail trail or bike path off the road that isn't specifically gravel).
Black - Closed road or private property. Do not enter!

Can I add photos?

• Yes! Photos with GPS data can be added through the Contribute menu. They will appear as camera icons on the map.
• Navigate to the Contribute tab and select the Upload Photo button.
• Upload as many photos as you like at a time.
